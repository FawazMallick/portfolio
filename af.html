Overview

As an Assistant Housefellow at Carnegie Mellon, I support a first-year residential community and work closely with student staff and Housing leadership to keep the day-to-day operations running smoothly while also making sure students feel supported and staff feel set up to succeed.

This role is a mix of leadership, operations, and people support, it’s not just one thing.

What I’ve done

  • Staff leadership & development: supported and guided a team of student staff, maintaining open communication and holding regular feedback conversations during scheduled 1:1's, guide staff strategic intitiatives with Community Advisor, such as programming goals for the upcoming semester, guide staff on spending and budget decisions, and ofcourse staff outing to bond and build team morale and rapport!
  • Conflict and crisis management: responded to situations involving interpersonal conflict, student wellbeing, and community disruptions, helping de-escalate issues and connecting students with appropriate campus resources when needed.
  • Coordination with campus departments: worked closely with Residential Education leadership and coordinated with other university offices when situations required broader support or follow-up.
  • Confidential information management: handled sensitive student information responsibly while maintaining professional boundaries and trust within the residential community.
  • Building and preparing staff teams: supported staffing processes including communicating with incoming RAs, confirming placements, and helping ensure the next year's team was organized and ready.
  • Operational and administrative support: helped manage house administrative processes including budgeting, staff coordination, and communication with Housing operations to keep the residential community running smoothly.

How I approach the role

  • Clear communication: set expectations early, follow up, and reduce confusion before it becomes a problem.
  • Operational reliability: handle details and deadlines so the team can focus on supporting students.
  • Iterative Approach & Flexibility: Things don't always work out the way you want it to, being flexible and being able to adapt my approach, is a skill I have devloped within this job
  • People-first leadership: keep things professional, calm, and respectful especially when issues are stressful.
