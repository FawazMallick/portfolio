AI-Based Fracture Detection

What?

This academic project aimed to use artificial intelligence to identify fractures in X-ray images and suggest optimal surgical intervention points. The focus was to replicate and improve the decision-making process of trained surgeons using machine learning, with the goal of enhancing diagnostic accuracy and pre-operative planning.

How?

We began by collecting a dataset of X-ray images showing various different fractures, and manually annotated the location of the fractures using a visual markup tool. These labeled images were then used to train a convolutional neural network using the ResNet-50 architecture in MATLAB.

After training, the model was tested by feeding it unseen X-rays and comparing its output predictions to expert annotations. The system was evaluated based on its ability to locate fracture sites and recommend incisions or fixation points. The AI model's performance was benchmarked using prediction overlap with human-marked regions.

Results

The model achieved high visual agreement with human predictions, demonstrating the potential of AI in assisting surgical planning. The side-by-side comparison of AI-predicted fracture locations and expert annotations showed strong alignment in both position and extent.
