Experience

Beyond Research — Leadership, Teaching, and Community

My engineering work doesn't happen in isolation. Alongside research and coursework, I've spent years in roles that required a different kind of skill: explaining complex ideas simply, managing communities under pressure, and building organizations from scratch. These experiences shaped how I think, communicate, and lead.

As a Community Assistant at Rowan, I was responsible for a residential floor of 300+ students — handling community programming, conflict resolution, and crisis response. This was my first sustained leadership experience, and it predated the CMU role by two years.

The CA role at Rowan is more hands-on at the resident level than the AF role at CMU — I was running programs, doing rounds, and handling direct student issues rather than managing staff. I learned the fundamentals of residential life work here.

  • Community programming: planned and executed floor events, floor community-building activities
  • First responder to student conflict, mental health concerns, and policy violations
  • Coordinated with Housing, Counseling Services, and Dean of Students on complex cases
  • Completed mandatory Title IX, mental health first aid, and crisis response training
Peer Tutor — Physics, Chemistry, Math Rowan University Aug 2023 – May 2025 · ~2 years

Tutored undergraduate students in introductory physics, chemistry, and mathematics. Subjects ranged from calculus-based mechanics to general chemistry problem sets to differential equations.

Tutoring forced a discipline I didn't expect: if you can't explain something clearly to someone who's struggling, you don't actually understand it. Every confused question revealed a gap in my own conceptual model. I got better at identifying which step in a chain of reasoning was failing, not just flagging that the answer was wrong.

  • Physics: kinematics, dynamics, energy methods, electrostatics, waves
  • Chemistry: stoichiometry, thermodynamics, electrochemistry, organic reactions
  • Math: calculus (single and multi-variable), linear algebra, differential equations
  • Adapted explanations to different learning styles — visual, algebraic, intuitive

The communication skills I built tutoring show up directly in my research work — in paper writing, poster presentations, and explaining Bayesian gait prediction to an audience that doesn't know what a prior distribution is.

Planetarium Presenter Rowan University Planetarium Aug 2021 – Feb 2023 · ~1.5 years

Operated and presented live planetarium shows to public audiences, school groups, and university visitors. Covered topics from basic astronomy (constellations, lunar cycles) to solar system mechanics, cosmology, and space exploration history.

The Rowan Planetarium hosts public shows, educational programs for K–12 students, and special events. As a presenter, I had to adapt content on the fly — a show for third graders is very different from a show for community adults or university faculty. This was my earliest experience with scientific communication to a non-technical audience.

  • Delivered live narrated shows under the dome for groups of 10–80 people
  • Operated Digistar projection system and dome controls during live presentations
  • Adapted content depth and vocabulary on the fly to audience age and background
  • Answered live Q&A — including the unexpected ones that required thinking fast
Chief Operating Officer — Mock Medical Device Company Rowan University — Senior Capstone 2023–2024

In Rowan's biomedical engineering senior capstone, students form a mock medical device company and operate it through the full product development lifecycle — from concept to regulatory submission. I served as COO, responsible for coordinating all technical and operational workstreams.

This meant owning project timelines, cross-team dependencies, design documentation, and regulatory deliverables (FDA submission format, ISO standard compliance). It was the first time I had to hold a team accountable to a schedule that wasn't mine — a fundamentally different experience from doing the technical work myself.

  • Coordinated between engineering, clinical, and regulatory workstreams
  • Managed project timeline and milestone deliverables across a multi-person team
  • Oversaw regulatory documentation: design history file, risk management per ISO 14971
  • Synthesized technical work from multiple team members into cohesive documentation

The regulatory thinking from this capstone directly carried into the exoskeleton project — the ISO 14971 risk analysis methodology I applied to the wearable device came from first learning it in this context.

The Thread

Looking at these roles together, a pattern emerges: I consistently sought out positions where I was responsible for other people — residents, students, team members — not just deliverables. That's not accidental. I believe the best engineers understand human systems as well as technical ones, and the only way to learn that is to actually be responsible for people.

The Planetarium taught me scientific communication. The tutoring taught me pedagogy. The CA/AF roles taught me leadership and crisis management. The COO role taught me operations. All of it feeds into how I approach research: explain clearly, work reliably, build systems that other people can use and trust.
