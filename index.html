The Journey

It started sophomore year. I had a simple question: "How do we help robots navigate complex spaces?"

That curiosity led me to my first research project — a 6-DOF surgical robot designed to assist in femur fracture realignment. I had no background in kinematics or configuration spaces. I learned fast. That steep learning curve, that combination of intense focus and perpetual confusion, taught me something fundamental: the best problems are the ones that seem impossible until you break them down.

That project changed my trajectory. It showed me that robotics and control systems weren't abstract mathematics — they were solutions to real-world problems. It led me to CMU. It shaped every project since.

Now, I focus on robotics that interacts directly with people. Prosthetic limbs that understand user intent. Wearable exoskeletons that prevent falls. Surgical systems that enhance human precision. The challenge isn't just solving the math — it's understanding humans, building reliable systems, and creating something that meaningfully improves lives.

What you'll find here: My research projects (from surgical robotics to gait prediction), the technical skills I've developed (control theory, machine learning, robotics fundamentals), and the leadership roles that shaped how I approach problems.
