Background

I completed my BS in Biomedical Engineering at Rowan University (Cum Laude, GPA 3.56/4.0, Dean's List) in May 2025, and I'm currently pursuing an MS in Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University (QPA 3.34/4.0, expected May 2027). My focus is human-centered robotics — systems that work in direct contact with people and need to be safe, reliable, and aware of human intent.

My research path started at Rowan. As a Research Associate at the Robossis Lab (Cooper Medical Center, PI: Dr. Mohammed Abedin-Nasab), I aided in the development of a path planning algorithm for a 6-DOF surgical robot — contributing to algorithm implementation and simulation validation for femur fracture realignment. That project introduced me to kinematic planning and clinical robotics.

From there I moved to wearable robotics. Back at Rowan (PI: Dr. Mitja Trkov), I contributed to the design and instrumentation of an FSR-based thigh brace for measuring human-exoskeleton interaction forces, and modeled the contact dynamics in OpenSim. That work was published at IFAC PapersOnLine 2025.

Now at CMU I'm a Graduate Research Assistant (PI: Dr. Hartmut Geyer), working on real-time foot placement prediction for prosthetic limb control. The focus is evaluating probabilistic and learning-based models for predicting human intent from IMU data — with current results reaching 29.3 cm RMSE and 376 ms of prediction lead time on over-ground walking. Actively exploring neural network architectures and hip-only sensing approaches.

Tools I work with: MATLAB / Simulink, Python (PyTorch, NumPy, Pandas, Scikit-learn), OpenSim, SolidWorks. Coursework spans robot dynamics, control theory, optimal control & RL, machine learning, deep learning, and medical device regulation.
