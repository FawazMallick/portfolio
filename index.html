About Me: My Journey

I’m Fawaz Mallick, a Biomedical Engineering graduate from Rowan University, and I’m currently pursuing an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University with a focus on robotic systems.

My background is built around robotics and real-world engineering work across mechanical, electrical, and biomedical domains. I’ve contributed to projects ranging from a 6-DOF surgical robot system for femur fracture realignment, where I developed a modified A* pathfinding approach, to a wearable exoskeleton project focused on stabilizing gait during slips and falls. I’ve also worked on an AI-based fracture detection project aimed at identifying fracture sites to support surgical planning.

Across these projects, I’ve worked with robotics fundamentals like kinematics, dynamics, modeling and simulation, and control, while also building practical engineering habits: breaking down messy problems into clear deliverables, documenting work, collaborating across roles, and adapting quickly when requirements change. I’m comfortable moving between research and implementation, whether that’s building and testing systems, working through technical literature, or translating an idea into something that can be evaluated with experiments.

At CMU, I balance multiple responsibilities: I serve as an Assistant Housefellow supporting a first-year residential community, I work as a Research Assistant in AI/ML-based human intent prediction, and I’m a full-time graduate student. I’m especially interested in robotics that interacts directly with people — assistive systems, rehabilitation, and medical robotics, where strong engineering has a direct impact on safety, usability, and outcomes.
