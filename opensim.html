OpenSim Gait Simulation

What?

This project leverages OpenSim to develop a biomechanical model of the interaction between a human subject and a lower limb exoskeleton device. The goal was to analyze forces at the contact points of the device, such as at the thigh or shank, and simulate the effectiveness of different exoskeleton designs, and to develop a real time controller in aiding movement and balance correction.

How?

The exoskeleton was first modeled in SolidWorks with simplified geometry. This CAD model was then exported and imported into OpenSim as a custom body attached to the standard human musculoskeletal model. Joint constraints were defined to simulate realistic behavior.

Contact forces between the exoskeleton and the human model were modeled using the "elastic foundation force" class in Opensim. This places a spring at key contact points, and the force is calculated based on the compression of the spring. To simulate real motion, IMU data collected from walking trials were read through Arduino and appended to the model. Ground truth data from force-sensitive resistors (FSRs) were used to validate the simulation output.

Results

Plots of interaction forces for different motions (walking, squatting, side-stepping, and stair-stepping) were generated from both the experimental FSRs and OpenSim simulations. These datasets were overlaid to compare trends and verify simulation fidelity. The results show that the simulation closely matches the experimental force patterns that were obtained
