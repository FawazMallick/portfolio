About Me

I'm a Biomedical Engineering graduate from Rowan University, currently pursuing an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University with a concentration in robotic systems.

My work spans mechanical, electrical, and biomedical domains. I've built a 6-DOF surgical robot with a custom A* motion planner, designed and fabricated a lower-limb exoskeleton, modeled human–exoskeleton dynamics in OpenSim, and trained CNNs for medical image analysis. I move fluently between algorithm design and physical prototyping.

My first publication appeared in IFAC PapersOnLine (2025), measuring interaction forces between humans and wearable exoskeletons using instrumented FSR sensors and OpenSim simulation — research directly relevant to safe, comfortable assistive robotics.

At CMU I balance full-time graduate study, a Research Assistant position in AI/ML-based human intent prediction, and leadership as an Assistant Housefellow in a first-year residential community.

Projects

Estimating Human–Exoskeleton Interaction Forces Using an Instrumented Thigh Brace & OpenSim

Published · IFAC 2025

Developed a wearable instrumented thigh brace with embedded FSRs to directly measure human–exoskeleton contact forces. Designed custom 3D-printed sensor housings, calibrated against a load frame, and ran dynamic walking trials with three subjects. Combined experimental measurements with an OpenSim contact simulation to estimate interaction forces during gait.

Outcomes: Captured measurable walking interface forces, revealed hip-motion correlations, and validated OpenSim simulation trends against FSR ground-truth data. Published in IFAC PapersOnLine (2025).

Robossis — 6-DOF Surgical Robot for Femur Fracture Realignment

Research

Designed and implemented a 6-DOF robotic surgical assistant (3 translational + 3 rotational axes). Developed a novel modified A* search algorithm in MATLAB operating over a 6D configuration space, integrated Bezier-curve smoothing for continuous trajectories, and modeled a spring-mass-damper haptic feedback system. Submitted to IEEE IROS.

Results: Simulated femur realignment achieved ≤3.0 mm translational deviation and ≤1.5° rotational deviation.

OpenSim Biomechanical Gait Simulation with Real-Time IMU Integration

Research

Built a full biomechanical simulation in OpenSim coupling a standard musculoskeletal model with a custom SolidWorks exoskeleton. Modeled contact via elastic foundation force elements, drove the simulation with live Arduino IMU gait data, and validated simulated forces against FSR ground-truth across four motion types.

Results: Simulation force profiles closely matched experimental FSR data for walking, squatting, side-stepping, and stair-stepping.

AI-Based Fracture Detection & Surgical Planning — ResNet-50 CNN

Academic

Built an end-to-end deep learning pipeline to identify fractures in X-ray images and recommend optimal surgical entry points. Manually annotated an X-ray fracture dataset, fine-tuned a ResNet-50 CNN in MATLAB, and benchmarked predictions against expert surgeon annotations.

Results: High visual agreement with expert annotations in both position and spatial extent — demonstrating AI viability for pre-operative surgical planning.
