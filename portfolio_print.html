★ Estimating Human–Exoskeleton Interaction Forces via Instrumented Thigh Brace & OpenSim

Published · IFAC 2025

Developed wearable FSR-embedded thigh brace, designed 3D-printed sensor housings, calibrated sensors with load frame, ran walking trials with three subjects, and built an OpenSim contact simulation. Combined data to characterize human–exoskeleton interaction forces during gait.

Published: IFAC PapersOnLine (2025). Captured walking interface forces, revealed hip-motion correlations, validated OpenSim trends vs. FSR ground truth.

Robossis — 6-DOF Surgical Robot for Femur Fracture Realignment

Research

Designed 6-DOF surgical robotic platform in MATLAB. Developed modified A* for 6D configuration-space pathfinding, Bezier-curve trajectory smoothing, and spring-mass-damper haptic feedback. Submitted to IEEE IROS.

Results: Simulated femur realignment with ≤3.0 mm translational and ≤1.5° rotational deviation.

OpenSim Biomechanical Gait Simulation with Real-Time IMU Integration

Research

Built OpenSim model coupling musculoskeletal human with SolidWorks exoskeleton. Modeled contact via elastic foundation forces. Drove simulation with live Arduino IMU data; validated against FSR ground truth across four motion types.

Results: Simulation closely matched experimental FSR data for walking, squatting, side-stepping, stair-stepping.
