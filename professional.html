Graduate Research Assistant
07/2025 – Present
Carnegie Mellon University · Robotics Institute · Pittsburgh, PA
PI: Dr. Hartmut Geyer — Human Intent Prediction for Control of Lower Limb Prosthetics

More than 2 million people in the United States live with lower-limb amputation, and falls remain the leading cause of injury among prosthetic users [1]. The core limitation of current devices is that they react — they sense ground contact and respond, leaving no time for proactive stability adjustments. My work in Dr. Geyer's lab asks: can we predict where the foot will land before it lands?

I am evaluating kinematic and inertial features — hip and knee angles, angular velocities, and IMU-derived signals from a thigh-mounted sensor — to determine which inputs most reliably discriminate intended foot placement at multiple walking speeds. In parallel, I am comparing deep learning architectures (LSTMs, temporal CNNs, and hybrid sequence models) for their ability to estimate foot placement in real time, weighing the trade-off between model complexity and the latency constraints of embedded prosthetic deployment.

Current Focus Areas

  • Feature selection: evaluating kinematic and inertial features as inputs to intent models across multiple walking speeds
  • Architecture benchmarking: exploring LSTM, temporal CNN, and Bayesian sequential estimators for foot placement prediction
  • Hip-only sensing: investigating whether intent can be reliably detected from a hip- or thigh-mounted IMU alone, avoiding the circular-sensing problem for prosthetic limb users
  • Latency analysis: quantifying inference time vs. prediction horizon trade-offs for embedded real-time control

Research Associate
08/2024 – 05/2025
Rowan University · Department of Mechanical Engineering · Glassboro, NJ
PI: Dr. Mitja Trkov — Design & Development of a Wearable Exoskeleton to Prevent Gait Perturbations

This research addressed a leading cause of injury in the elderly and mobility-impaired: loss of balance during gait perturbations. Working with Dr. Trkov's lab, I contributed to the design, instrumentation, simulation, and publication of a wearable thigh brace equipped with eight force-sensitive resistors (FSR-406) to detect human–exoskeleton interaction forces in real time.

Contributions

  • Sensor integration: contributed to the design of FSR-406 sensor housings and brace components; calibrated sensors to R²=0.9972 using an exponential model across the full 0–20 N range
  • OpenSim simulation: modeled human–exoskeleton contact dynamics using elastic foundation force contact (stiffness 60,000 N/m; damping 3,000 N·s/m); achieved ≈85% validation accuracy against experimental force measurements
  • IMU integration: interfaced BNO055 IMU (100 Hz) with Arduino Uno (150 Hz) for kinematic capture to inform musculoskeletal simulation inputs
  • CAD & prototyping: used SolidWorks CAD to help prototype parts for the exoskeleton; contributed to brainstorming and iterative design of brace components
  • Regulatory & risk analysis: investigated relevant regulatory standards and contributed to the risk mitigation analysis for the wearable device
  • Publication: co-authored peer-reviewed IFAC PapersOnLine paper (2025); DOI: 10.1016/j.ifacol.2025.12.262

📄 Publication: Varma, Roberts, Mallick, Trkov. "Estimating Human-Exoskeleton Interaction Forces Using an Instrumented Thigh Brace and OpenSim." IFAC PapersOnLine, Vol. 59-30 (2025), pp. 353–358.

Research Associate

Robossis · Cooper University Hospital · Camden, NJ
PI: Dr. Mohammed Abedin Nasab — Development of a Fracture Reduction Algorithm for 6-DOF Surgical Robot

Femur fractures require precise anatomical realignment before internal fixation; manual reduction is imprecise, radiation-intensive, and physically demanding for the surgical team. In this role I developed the path planning and guidance algorithm for the Robossis RSS surgical platform, a 6-DOF parallel robot designed for orthopaedic procedures at Cooper University Hospital.

Technical Work

  • Algorithm development: aided in the development and implementation of a robotics-aligned path-finding algorithm in MATLAB for 6D configuration space (three translational + three rotational DOF)
  • Simulation & benchmarking: implemented and tested the algorithm on a midshaft femur fracture scenario — achieving ≤3.0 mm / ≤1.5° final reduction accuracy in simulation
  • Validation support: contributed to simulation testing and result analysis on the Robossis RSS platform at Cooper University Hospital
